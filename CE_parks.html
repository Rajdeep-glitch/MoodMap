The Victoria Memorial is an iconic marble building located in Kolkata, India, built between 1906 and 1921. It was constructed in honor of Queen Victoria, commemorating her 25-year reign over India. The design, conceived by Sir William Emerson, blends classical European architecture with Mughal elements, resulting in a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic revivalist style. The memorial is surrounded by lush gardens and houses a museum that displays an extensive collection of British colonial-era artifacts, paintings, and sculptures. Today, the Victoria Memorial stands as a symbol of Kolkata's rich historical and cultural heritage, attracting millions of visitors annually.
